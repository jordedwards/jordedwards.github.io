Cruise Line Application

Supporting one of the largest cruise line's world-wide, performance and reliability were two critical factors of this application's success. I worked with a small team on the middleware (written in .NET) of this project to support all of the request traffic between the iOS and Android mobile applications to the back-end services. By optimizing caching at both the controller and web-service levels, and re-writing numerous requests to run asynchronously, response time of key features in this application such as the homescreen were improved by as much as 227%. This project was a great opportunity to learn how to handle high-volume request traffic and provide a world-class mobile experience for thousands of concurrent users.

Insurance Questionnaire

This tool provides a simple questionnaire for users to take in order to determine whether or not they are eligible for particular insurance plans, and more specifically which plans are most recommended to them based on factors such as age and physical health. An important consideration of this project was that it must be accessible to all potential users including those with physical disabilities such as visual or hearing impairments. This questionnaire was designed to meet WCAG 2.0 AA accessibility standards.

Pharmaceutical Shipment Management

This application was built for our client to effectively create, manage, and track large orders of pharmaceutical supplies across Canada. Some key features include a dashboard (shown in the image) which highlights geographical areas which is sending/receiving a large volume of shipments. A major focus of this application was to provide a very fluent and intuitive user experience, as it would be used by hundreds of medical staff across Canada.
